Being smart is a kind of knowledge

Benjamin Shen
2022-10-01
Knowledge

Think to be smart

Mode of thinking is a kind of knowledge, and everybody can become ‘wiser’ by studying and practicing. Human brain capacity, or so the capacity human can manipulate, is limited, while the complexity of the external world is almost unlimited. So a human can only understand the external world approximately, which leads to cognitive limitations.

Cognitive limitations

There are two main types of limitations: People don’t know the thing or mistakenly infer it from established cognitive structures. Moreover, some irrational people get stuck in the past and refuse to learn anything new. Fortunately, there are lots of universal similarities between things. We can know the world by mastering the way of understanding things and inferring the world from part of it. When we think of concepts, we should not think of them at an abstract but at a concrete level. For example, speaking of triangles, we certainly will conjure up an image of a triangle. Speak of triangle interior angle summation theorem, the three angles in a triangle will piece together in our mind.

How to think?

The concept of triangles is certainly produced by concreteness. It is nothing until it’s connected with concreteness. Someone who can recite the literal form of mathematical theorems will never have the image in mind, and the concept can never be understood or thought deeply. The concept is nothing itself, but the universal set of concreteness makes it up. Some people use linguistic derivation with ideas only to reach an abstract conclusion. They are laboring under the impression that they are talking much. For example, when two people discuss philosophy, their understanding may vary. But the problem is that their linguistics usage is the same, while the ways different people think of it are different.

Common mistakes

A vacuous way of thinking seems to lead to a conclusion that appears to be reasonable, even exactly correct conclusion. But the thinker has no idea of the correctness of the deduction. He uses the rules of the linguistic game to derive a conclusion but fails to understand it. The proper concept usage is to use the base, the concrete aspects of the concept, to start concept deduction. At least, he should verify a conclusion that seems correct in every concrete aspect.

Subjectivity and solution

On the one hand, understanding of the world is generated from a subject. A person, as a subject, understands an object, as a result of which, any understanding includes an imprint from its subject. We don’t know. We just believe that how we recognize things is aligned with what they are, which leads to a complete avowal. We cannot always remain skeptical, and neither can we trust in everything. The former behavior proves costly, and the latter is an invalid simplification.

On the other hand, the difference between subjects can result in different understandings. After which, we try to think from the viewpoint of others, find out the difference between our cognitive evolution and theirs and reach the fundamental reason that brings about the argument. That is to say, two rational and honest people will necessarily settle because one must have some misunderstanding, and they find it. Once others and I have different opinions, I tend to analyze our understandings and find the inconsistency in one concept that results in the inconsistency in understanding. After we clarify the concept, we settle.

Conclusion

The truth is abstract and simple, but a simple concept contains much concreteness.
